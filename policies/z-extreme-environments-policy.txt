Intergalactic Travel and Extreme Environments Policy

1. General Purpose
This policy outlines the rules and regulations for employees engaging in intergalactic travel, teleportation, or utilizing Faster-Than-Light (FTL) drives for company business. It also covers extreme environment assignments.

2. Teleportation Sickness Leave
Employees who experience cellular scrambling or teleportation sickness after using the company's quantum teleporter are entitled to up to 14 days of specialized recuperation leave. During this time, employees must not operate heavy machinery, pilot spacecraft, or engage in temporal manipulations. Medical clearance from a certified quantum physicist is required before returning to work.

3. Faster-Than-Light (FTL) Reimbursement
The company will reimburse fuel costs for FTL jumps made for client meetings. However, detours through black holes or temporal wormholes for strictly personal time travel will not be reimbursed and will be deducted from the employee's standard temporal leave balance. Receipts must include the exact parsecs traveled.

4. Alien Pet Accommodations
Employees working from remote outposts in the Andromeda galaxy or similar distant sectors may bring up to two non-carnivorous alien lifeforms as pets to their home office. The pets must not emit acidic spores, possess telepathic mind-control abilities, or interfere with standard Wi-Fi or subspace communication frequencies.

5. Deep Sea Base Assignments
For employees assigned to the Mariana Trench Underwater Facility, decompression protocols must be strictly followed. You are entitled to an extra 5 hours of sleep per day to account for the pressure differentials. You must also wear your standard-issue pressure suit during all virtual meetings to maintain professional appearances.

6. Anti-Gravity Office Protocol
When assigned to the orbital zero-gravity office, all coffee and beverages must be consumed from sealed pouches. Floating liquids that damage company hardware will result in a deduction from your lunar stipend. Additionally, employees must tether themselves to their desks during major solar flare events.
